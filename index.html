Fractional investments allow you to invest in a portion of an asset, such as real estate or high-value properties, with a smaller initial investment compared to full ownership.

Micro-investments allow you to invest small amounts into real estate properties. These investments grow over time as part of a larger pool of investors, offering access to premium properties at a fraction of the cost.

You can invest in a variety of properties such as residential buildings, commercial spaces, and vacation homes. Fractional ownership enables shared access to these high-value assets.

The duration of a fractional investment varies depending on the terms of the investment. It can range from a few months to several years, depending on the property and investment type.

Micro-investment platforms allow you to start investing with as little as $100, making it accessible to investors who may not have large sums of money to commit upfront.

Like any investment, fractional ownership carries risks, such as market fluctuations, property value changes, and liquidity issues. It's essential to assess these risks before committing your funds.
